Gaming in a soundscape

Master thesis

December 10, 2020

Resume

Video games, by allowing users to evolve in multisensory data spaces, offer an unrivaled form of experience. In these universes to explore, the sound dimension occupies a preponderant place, as it strongly impacts immersiveness.

This dissertation proposes to study the sound as a medium present in video games through a holistic approach (see R.M. Schafer). A first part will be dedicated to define this medium and its specificities, to subsequently determine its functions and impacts on the user, in order, ultimately, to isolate new perspectives for further innovation and experimentation.
